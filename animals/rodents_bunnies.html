Rats and Mice are both fantastic indoor pets. They require minimal care, although rats can smell if not washed often. They are happy to be left with themselves and don't require human interaction, as they are both social and isolated animals depending on their environment.

Guinea Pigs and Rabbits are pets that are suitible for indoors, but not recommended as it can be far too cramped unless you have happy to have them walking around freely. Large outdoor cages make both of these very happy! Rabbits are quiet and cautious whereas Guinea Pigs are largely vocal and enjoy social interaction with other animals and people. Both are very easy to feed, only requireing some hard meal or birdseed, some leafy greens from the produce section at the supermarket, and possibly a salt lick to keep their sodium levels nice and healthy!
